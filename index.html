Welcome to our Targeted Editing series where today you hang out a sign that says "Do Not Disturb", or make OpenStreetMap data superb! (both take about the same amount of time)

  1. How are addresses used?

    1. Help with search. When you plug an address into the search bar on a map, your result is dependent on the underlying data. Sometimes the address data is only present at the start and end of a street segment. So if someone searched for 550 Elm Street, a pin may fall right in the middle of the Elm Street segment that represents all of the addresses between 500 Elm Street and 600 Elm Street. This is known as interpolation. This might not be accurate, though. It assumes that all of the addresses are evenly spaced. With address points or addresses associated to actual buildings, you can get a pin drop in a much more precise location.

    2. Help with routing. How many times have you generated a route that cheerfully announces “You have arrived” when the building you are looking for is still a few hundred feet away? If the building itself had an associated address, you might get that cheerful message at a more appropriate time.
